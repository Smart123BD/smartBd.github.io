// 02 — Technology

How Piezoelectric
Energy Harvesting Works

Mechanical pressure from passing vehicles generates electrical energy through crystalline materials embedded in infrastructure.

PIEZOELECTRIC ENERGY CONVERSION FLOW DIAGRAM

STEP 01🚛

Mechanical Stress

Vehicles, trains, and pedestrians apply mechanical pressure to the road or bridge surface.

STEP 02💡

Crystal Activation

Piezoelectric crystals (PZT/PVDF) embedded in the pavement deform and generate a voltage differential.

STEP 03🔌

AC to DC Conversion

Rectifier circuits convert alternating output to stable direct current for storage or grid use.

STEP 04🌎

Distribution

Energy flows into batteries, powers roadside lighting, or feeds directly into the national grid.

♻

100% Renewable

Harnesses kinetic energy already present in traffic — no fuel, no combustion, no emissions.

📈

Fully Scalable

Modular tile-based systems install on any road or bridge length, scaling output proportionally.

🔧

Low Maintenance

No moving parts above the road surface. Crystal arrays sealed beneath load-bearing pavement panels.

🧠

Smart and IoT-Ready

Integrated sensors monitor energy output, traffic patterns, and structural health in real time.

🌙

24/7 Generation

Unlike solar, piezoelectric systems generate power day and night, rain or shine, whenever traffic flows.

🏛

Smart City Ready

Sensor data powers traffic management, smart lighting, and emergency response systems.

// 03 — Applications

Where the Technology Lives

Four primary infrastructure domains where SIBC deploys piezoelectric harvesting systems across Bangladesh.

🛣

Roads and National Highways

Bangladesh's national highway network handles millions of vehicles daily. Embedding piezoelectric modules beneath asphalt on high-traffic corridors like the Dhaka–Chittagong Highway generates electricity continuously and passively, powering roadside lighting, signage, and EV charging points locally.

Dhaka-Chittagong N1Dhaka-Sylhet N2Up to 47kW/kmGrid-Fed
🌇

Bridges and Underpass Spans

Bridge decks experience intense vibration from vehicle load and natural frequencies. SIBC installs piezoelectric arrays along bridge surfaces and expansion joints, generating power while simultaneously monitoring structural stress via embedded sensors across Bangladesh's major bridge network.

Meghna BridgeKarnaphuli BridgeVibration HarvestStructural Monitoring
🚧

Toll Booths and Plazas

Toll booths are natural chokepoints where vehicles slow down, creating dense repeated load cycles. This concentration of mechanical energy makes toll plazas among the most efficient locations for piezoelectric harvesting, with rapid ROI and minimal disruption to existing infrastructure.

Expressway TollsHigh Load DensityFast ROISelf-Sufficient Booths
🚂

Railways and Transport Corridors

Rail infrastructure generates enormous vibrational energy with every passing train. Heavy locomotives exert tremendous consistent force on rails and sleepers. SIBC's rail-integrated systems capture this energy to power station lighting, signalling systems, and wayside communication equipment.

// 04 — Why Bangladesh Needs This

The Right Solution
at the Right Time

  • ✓

    Rapid Infrastructure Expansion

    Bangladesh is building thousands of km of roads, bridges, and railways under Vision 2041 — the ideal time to integrate harvesting systems from the ground up.

  • ✓

    High Traffic Density

    Dhaka is among the world's most congested cities. High vehicle density directly translates to higher energy yield from piezoelectric systems.

  • ✓

    Energy Security Imperative

    Bangladesh faces growing electricity demand with a significant gap in renewable supply. Road-embedded generation reduces dependence on fossil fuel imports.

  • ✓

    Smart City Development

    Government smart city initiatives in Dhaka, Chittagong, and Sylhet require integrated power and data infrastructure — exactly what SIBC provides.

  • ✓

    Climate Vulnerability

    As one of the most climate-vulnerable nations, Bangladesh's commitment to clean energy aligns perfectly with zero-emission piezoelectric systems.
